About My Practice

I wish to provide you with high-quality, personalized psychiatric care and want to work with you collaboratively on the problems you wish to address. The following information may be helpful to you in understanding how I work and how you can make the most of your treatment.

General Information

I offer treatment for anxiety and depression problems, utilizing various forms of psychotherapy and medications when indicated. I try to keep the therapy understandable and practical. The therapy process takes time, by its nature, but I try to be active from the start in giving you ideas and feedback about your situation. I focus on the importance of relationships and interactions with other people, and how these relate to disturbances in mood. I pay attention to how your past experience influences the way you view your present situation. I also use some cognitive therapy concepts, which involve identifying negative thinking patterns and correcting these. If therapy by itself does not seem to be providing sufficient relief of your symptoms, we will talk about the use of medications.

It is important that we meet at least once or twice a week at the beginning of treatment so that I can get to know you and understand the problems you are struggling with. Anything that is on your mind when you come to the session may be useful for us to discuss. As the treatment progresses, we can evaluate together how frequently we should meet, in order to achieve your goals and to allow for continued improvement.

If you have questions or concerns about your treatment experience with me, please bring these up during your session. It will aid our working together if you can be very honest about what has been helpful to you and what has not been helpful.

Payment/Insurance

I ask that you be responsible for paying me directly for my services. I participate with CDPHP, MVP, UBH and NYS Empire Plan; for these plans you will owe a co-payment at each visit. Some insurance plans will reimburse participants for a portion of my fee.
