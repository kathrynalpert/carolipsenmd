Notice of Privacy Practices

Notice of Privacy Practices for Protected Health Information

This notice describes how medical information about you may be used and disclosed and how you can get access to this information. Please review it carefully.

I am required by law to keep a record of your treatment. Information about your treatment will ordinarily not be released without your written consent or authorization. Even with your written permission, I generally send only a treatment summary with relevant information and do not release the entire record.

Federal privacy laws now require that you be informed of possible uses and disclosures of your health information. Listed below are possible uses of your healthcare information for treatment purposes, for payment purposes and for healthcare operations. I will ask you to sign a form acknowledging that you are aware of these possible disclosures. You may request restrictions on the uses of your healthcare information if you wish; I will let you know whether I can accommodate your request.

Examples of use of your healthcare information for treatment purposes would be:

  • I record information in your chart about your treatment.
  • I consult with another physician or specialist to obtain his/her input on a problem that you are having.
  • I phone in a prescription to a pharmacy at your request.
  • I leave a reminder on your answering machine or with a family member about an upcoming appointment.

Examples of use of your healthcare information for payment purposes:

  • The insurance company requests a diagnosis and procedure code in order to process a claim and I provide that information to them.
  • I fill out a treatment plan that the insurance company requires in order to provide coverage of your treatment.

Example of use of your healthcare information for healthcare operations:

  • I obtain services from my insurers and consultants such as quality assessment, quality improvement, outcome evaluation, credentialing, medical review, legal services, and insurance. I may share information about you with such insurers or consultants as necessary to obtain these services. Whenever possible, this information would be shared without any identifying information.

Other possible disclosures would include:

  • Using my best judgment, I may disclose to a family member or a close personal friend (or any other person you identify) health information relevant to that person's involvement in your care or in payment for such care if you do not object or in an emergency.
  • To avert a serious danger to health or safety, I may disclose your protected health information to prevent or lessen a serious, imminent threat to the health or safety of a person or the public.
  • I am required to report child abuse and neglect to public authorities by NYS law.
  • As required by law, I may disclose your protected health information to public and legal authorities charged with preventing or controlling disease, injury or disability.
  • I may disclose information in the course of any judicial or administrative proceeding as allowed or required by law, with your consent, or as directed by proper court order.
  • I may disclose to the FDA your protected health information regarding an adverse event with respect to food, supplements, or product defects to enable recalls.

Any other disclosures will be only as required by law, or will be at your specific request and will require that you sign a specific authorization form. Examples of these latter disclosures would be:

  • You wish me to provide information regarding your diagnosis and treatment to a new physician with whom you will be entering treatment.
  • You wish me to provide information to an insurance company so they can issue a policy for you.

Your Health Information Rights

The health and billing records I maintain are the physical property of my office. The information in it, however, belongs to you. You have the right to:

  • Request a restriction on the uses and disclosures of your health information by delivering the request in writing to me. (I am not required to grant the request, unless the requested restriction involves information to be sent to a health plan for payment of health care operations purposes and the disclosure relates to products or services that were paid for solely out-of-pocket and such disclosure is not required by law.) I will comply with any request granted.
  • Obtain a paper copy of the Notice of Privacy Practices for Protected Health Information, by asking me for this.
  • Request that you be allowed to inspect and copy your health and billing record, by delivering a written request to me and scheduling a time with me to review your record in my office.
  • Appeal a denial of access to your protected health information.
  • Request that your health care record be amended to correct incomplete or incorrect information by delivering a written request to me. (I am not required to make such amendments.)
  • File a statement of disagreement if your amendment is denied, and require that the request for the amendment and the denial be attached in all future disclosures of your protected health information.
  • Obtain an accounting of disclosures of your health information as required to be maintained by law. An accounting will not include internal uses of information for treatment, payment, or operations, disclosures made to you or made at your request, or disclosures made to family members or friends in the course of providing care.
  • Request that communication of your health information be made by alternate means or at an alternative location by delivering that request in writing to me.
  • Revoke authorizations that you made previously to use or disclose information except to the extent information or action has already been taken by delivering a written revocation to me.

If you want to exercise any of the above rights, please contact me in person or in writing during normal hours. I will give you assistance in how to exercise these rights. Additionally, if you believe your privacy rights have been violated, you may file a written complaint to me. You may also file a complaint by mailing it or e-mailing it to the Secretary of Health and Human Services. I will not retaliate against you for filing such a complaint.

You have the right to review this Notice before signing the consent authorizing use and disclosure of your protected health information for treatment, payment and healthcare operations purposes.

Notice of Breach of Health Information

In the unlikely event that your health information is inadvertently acquired, accessed, used by or disclosed to an unauthorized person, I will provide you with written notice of such breach. The notice will be sent without unreasonable delay and in no case later than 60 calendar days after discovery of a breach. The notice will be written in plain language and will contain the following information: (i) a brief description of what happened, the date of the breach, if known, and the date of discovery; (ii) the type of PHI involved in the breach; (iii) any precautionary steps you should take; (iv) a description of what I am doing to investigate and mitigate the breach and prevent future breaches; and (v) how you may contact me to discuss the breach.

The written notice of breach will be sent by regular mail. If the contact information I maintain for you is insufficient or out-of-date, I may attempt to provide notice to you by telephone or other permissible alternate method. I will also report the breach to the U.S. Department of Health and Human Services.

My Responsibilities

My office is required to:

  • Maintain the privacy of your health information as required by law,
  • Provide you with a notice as to my duties and privacy practices as to the information I collect and maintain about you,
  • Abide by the terms of this Notice,
  • Notify you if I cannot accommodate a requested restriction or request, and
  • Accommodate your reasonable requests regarding methods to communicate health information.

I reserve the right to amend, change, or eliminate provisions in my privacy practices and access practices and to enact new provisions regarding the protected health information. If my practice changes, I will amend my Notice. You are entitled to receive a revised copy of the Notice by calling and requesting a copy.

If you have questions or would like additional information, please contact me, Dr. Carol Ipsen, at 518-439-5624.
